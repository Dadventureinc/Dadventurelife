10 Things I’ve Learned From Traveling the World That I Want to Teach My Kids

Reflections from a clockwise journey through Morocco.

I recently hit country number thirty-something. It’s a strange milestone. On one hand, I’ve seen a large part of the world. On the other hand, I’ve barely scratched the surface.

The reason I travel has evolved over time. It’s no longer just about places—it’s layered, interconnected. Like a map that keeps expanding the more you explore it. Certain moments light up brighter than others. Small, seemingly insignificant points in time that stay with you long after you’ve left.

"Moments that never would have existed if I had stayed home."

The Blue Pearl and the Kindness of Fez

This time, it was a clockwise road trip through Morocco. I started in Casablanca and made my way north to Chefchaouen—the “Blue Pearl.” The celebration of Eid al-fitr felt like a new years party. People filled the narrow blue walkways from every corner of the town.

From there, I went to Fez. Dense, chaotic, alive. I spent two days with a local guide named Abby. At one point Abby stops to buy cheese for a hungry street cat. It was a beautiful moment of love and kindness. One that gave me assurance that goodness can be found in all corners of the planet.

The Lessons

1. Travel

There is more to see than you will ever be able to experience in one lifetime. The more you travel, the more you realize how much you haven’t seen. Go anyway.

2. Show Respect

You are always a guest. Respect the people. Respect the culture. The way you carry yourself matters more than you think.

3. Be Yourself

You will never blend in completely—and you don’t need to. You’re there to experience the world, not pretend you belong to it.

4. Know Where You’re Going

You don’t need a rigid plan—but you do need direction. Have a sense of where you are, where you’re headed, and why.

5. Pay Attention

Learn to tell the difference between those who see you as an opportunity and those who genuinely want to help. Trust your instincts.

6. Learn the Basics

Learn a few words: Hello, Please, Thank you, Nice to meet you, Cheers! Kindness translates everywhere.

7. Be Prepared

You can’t prepare for everything—but you can prepare for a lot. A map. A backup plan. A few essentials.

8. Don’t Get Attached

Plans will change. Things will go wrong. Be flexible enough to let the unexpected happen.

9. You Are an Ambassador

Whether you realize it or not, you represent your family and your values. Every interaction is a reflection of who you are.

10. You Can Handle More Than You Think

Wherever you are—whether it’s across the world or down the street—you are capable of figuring it out. Adapting. Learning. Growing.

Travel won’t give you all the answers. But it will teach you how to ask better questions.
